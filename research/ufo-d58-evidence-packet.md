## Bottom Line

`D58` is the highest-value unresolved document-only target in the current range-fouler set. It is valuable because the public form preserves a dense operational chain: KINGPIN-directed identification of an unknown contact, radar lock, target-pod video, two IR-significant contacts, a reported closest range of `16.9NM`, red blinking strobes, and noise-jamming indications.

It is also one of the strongest conventional-confound cases. Red strobes, jamming indications, radar lock, target-pod video, and a directed ID attempt are all compatible with conventional military aircraft, drones, training/range activity, or electronic-warfare platforms. The public release does not include the radar data, target-pod video, EW details, platform identity, FOV, range-time series, or unredacted location needed to resolve those lanes.

Treat D58 as a source-request target, not as public proof of exotic performance.

## Claim Tiers

### Directly Preserved

- D58 is a range-fouler form with a detection on `10/27/20` at `01:12:21Z`.
- The event occurred at night during a `DCA` mission.
- The form records two contacts in the group.
- The contact altitude field reads `26000`, altitude constant is marked `No`, and movement is marked `Yes`.
- The stable trackfile field is marked `Yes`.
- The narrative reports KINGPIN-directed identification, radar lock, and target-pod video.
- The narrative reports two IR-significant contacts and a closest approach no better than `16.9NM`.
- The narrative reports two red blinking strobes and noise jamming indicated by two chevrons.

### Lower-Confidence Source/Form Fields

- The direction/speed field reads `060/20`, but public context is insufficient to convert it into a validated trajectory or speed claim.
- The shape/appearance checkbox area appears to mark `Balloon-shaped`, `Metallic`, `Opaque`, `Reflective`, and `Other Shape`, but OCR and checkbox extraction are noisy. Use the narrative and core operational fields ahead of these checkboxes.
- The relative-behavior phrase about one contact circling the other is important, but the apparent timing in OCR is not reliable enough for turn-rate, acceleration, or exotic-motion claims.

### Missing Or Redacted

- Unredacted contact latitude and longitude.
- Platform identity, aircraft position, and platform motion.
- Radar returns, track history, and radar mode details.
- Target-pod video, still frames, sensor FOV/zoom state, gimbal pointing, and range-time series.
- EW receiver details, jammer type, frequency, bearing, or waveform.
- KINGPIN communications or tasking transcript.
- Contact classification outcome after the directed ID attempt.
- Any public DVIDS/PR video page explicitly paired to `DoW-UAP-D58`.

## Analytic Controls

Evaluate D58 against these conventional lanes before any exotic interpretation:

1. Military aircraft operating with strobes, radar detectability, and electronic emissions.
2. Drone or remotely piloted aircraft activity with IR signatures and visible strobes.
3. Electronic-warfare or training activity creating noise-jamming indications.
4. Two separate conventional contacts whose apparent circling relationship is caused by sensor geometry, platform motion, or track update artifacts.
5. Range activity or friendly/coalition operations not visible in the redacted public form.

The strongest reason to keep D58 high is not that it defeats these lanes. It is that the public form preserves enough operational detail to make the missing source material specific and testable.

## Unsupported Claims

Do not use D58 to claim:

- non-human technology
- impossible acceleration or instantaneous circling
- confirmed physical orbiting between two objects
- validated `060/20` speed/heading
- a hard match to `PR44`, `PR42`, `PR47`, or any `DOD_*.mp4`
- a link to local Greece `D35` based on the PDF metadata issue

## Source-Request Checklist

Priority source requests:

1. Target-pod video for the full interaction around `10/27/20 01:12:21Z`.
2. Radar track data for the stable trackfile, including range, bearing, altitude, closure, and track quality.
3. EW/noise-jamming data showing the two-chevron indication and any receiver metadata.
4. KINGPIN tasking or communications around the directed ID.
5. Platform position, altitude, speed, heading, and sensor pointing during the contact.
6. Unredacted bullseye or coordinate context for `B/E ZIM 248/17`.
7. Post-event identification, if any, from squadron intelligence or range-control follow-up.
8. Any official PR/DVIDS release page that explicitly names `DoW-UAP-D58`, `D58`, `10/27/20`, `01:12:21Z`, `16.9NM`, `noise jamming`, or `KINGPIN`.
