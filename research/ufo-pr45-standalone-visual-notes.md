## Findings

The visual evidence supports PR45 as a distinct standalone clip. The strongest local support is not the early small-return interval; it is the `35.0s-57.0s` window, where every sampled row from the growth interval and most of the late interval carries a high-or-medium central contrast detection.

The apparent contrast area increases materially in the late sequence. Median detected area rises from about `52.5 px` in the initial interval to `204.0 px` in the growth interval and `425.0 px` in the late interval, with a late maximum of `2168 px`.

The late rows show the contrast area moving down and right from the reticle/track center. The widened pass preserves this transition through the lower-right exit-adjacent frames: rows around `56.0s-58.4s` shift from high/medium to low as the object/contrast complex moves away from the reticle center and partly toward the lower crop boundary.

The early rows remain weak. In the `0.0s-17.9s` interval, most detections are low or none because the visible evidence is small and reticle-confounded. Do not use the early rows as independent object-shape or object-size evidence.

## Interpretation

PR45 is now validated locally as a meaningful standalone visual-interest clip. It shows a tracked central contrast area, growth in apparent size/area, and lower-right exit-adjacent motion in the public video.

It should not be promoted to a physical kinematics case. The public MP4 does not expose range, FOV, platform motion, zoom state, slant angle, target coordinates, altitude, size, or speed. The apparent growth can be caused or amplified by platform closure, sensor zoom, contrast settings, or a true target-size/apparent-size change; the public clip alone cannot separate those mechanisms.

It should also not be forced into the D44/D56/D58 range-fouler report cluster. No hard `D` report pairing has been found for PR45.
