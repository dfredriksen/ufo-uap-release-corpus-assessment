## Working Title

Scientific Assessment Of The May 2026 UFO/UAP Release Corpus: Operational Records, Public Media, Evidence Limits, And Source-Request Priorities

## Report Positioning

The report should be written as a scientific assessment of released records, not as an advocacy document. It should distinguish:

- source-reported observations from independently verified facts
- image-plane motion from physical motion
- official release identity from inferred report-content match
- unresolved status from extraordinary origin
- modern operational evidence from historical/archive context

## Proposed Structure

### 1. Abstract

One-page summary:

- corpus size and scope
- key finding: credible unresolved operational observations
- strongest evidence lanes
- limits: no public proof of non-human technology or independent public kinematic reconstruction
- highest-value source requests

### 3. Methods

Cover:

- file inventory and duplicate handling
- PDF text extraction and OCR where needed
- DVIDS/War.gov source-index reconciliation
- video contact sheets, phase review, dense/manual tracks, and geometry feasibility

Required caveat:

Public video analysis cannot recover true object kinematics without FOV/zoom state, range, platform motion, gimbal pointing, and telemetry.

### 9. Scientific Limitations

Required points:

- no raw telemetry
- missing FOV/zoom state
- missing slant range and sensor pointing
- missing platform motion
- redactions and OCR noise
- source-index inconsistencies
- report-derived speeds/shapes vs public-media-derived observations
- limited depth on historical archive/image material

### 10. Source-Request Priorities

Top requests:

1. D58 target-pod video, radar data, EW/noise-jamming logs, KINGPIN communications, and platform identity constraints.
2. D28 raw MX-20/MX-25 imagery and weapon-release/impact timeline.
3. D33 and D38 telemetry/FOV/slant range/platform/gimbal data.
4. D25 SWIR/raw sensor metadata and release-label correction.
5. D27 PR29 source-index correction and raw sensor/video data.
6. D61/D65 raw FMV, DGS-1 notes, unredacted grids, and platform/sensor metadata.

### 11. Conclusions

Expected conclusion:

- The corpus materially supports unresolved operational reporting.
- The strongest cases justify source requests and further official clarification.
- The public material does not support a conclusion of non-human technology.
- The best next scientific step is raw-data acquisition, not stronger interpretation of compressed public media.

## Drafting Rules

- Use "the source reports" when referring to document claims.
- Use "local review supports" only for observations actually produced by local visual/OCR analysis.
- Do not convert image-plane tracks into physical tracks.
- Do not collapse PR labels into D-report labels without official support.
- Do not infer shape, speed, altitude, range, or origin from redacted or missing fields.
- State that "unresolved" is not equivalent to "extraordinary."
